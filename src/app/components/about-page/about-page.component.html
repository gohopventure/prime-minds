About

I am a licensed clinical social worker with over five years of experience working with individuals and families from various stages of life. I completed my academic training in Grambling, Louisiana, at Grambling State University, where I earned a master’s degree in social work. I am trained to work with various clinical issues such as trauma, anxiety, depression, and other related issues. Additionally, I have extensive experience working with adults diagnosed with severe and persistent mental illness. I have experience providing crisis stabilization, community-based mental health, substance abuse, hospice, and assessment and diagnostic services.

Having had personal experiences in the foster care system, adoption, and other life traumas, I believe narratives can change and overcome life-altering circumstances. My passion has always been and continues to be working with individuals who have encountered challenging life situations and are attempting to become a better version of themselves and realize that “Every Mind has a Prime.”
